About Me



Hi, I'm Noah.

I grew up in Pittsburgh [The City of Champions], and proudly represent it everywhere I go. Pittsburgh was built on values of a strong work ethic and philanthropy, which I wholeheartedly stand behind. When the time came to move off to school, I chose to attend the University of Waterloo for it's Management Engineering and Co-Op Programs.

At UWaterloo we have the chance to complete 6 different internships over the course of our program. Co-Op has been extremely valuable and while it has taught me countless lessons, it has also allowed me to find what kinds of things I enjoy at different companies and in different roles. Specifically, I've found that I really enjoy working in the fast pace and ever changing tech world and have gravitated towards PM roles. I used to say I didn't know what I wanted to do and didn't want to pick a single role, but as a PM being involved in all aspects of a project/product, I've found that maybe I don't have to pick, I can be involved in everything.

When I'm not in the classrooom, you can likely find me working for various student organizations. I love building and leading teams, and specifically the challenges that come with each. In my free time depending on the season, I'll either be cheering on my favorite hockey team or trying to sneak in a round of golf. I'm also an avid reader of all things in the business, leadership and self development genres.

Education

Managment Engineering at the University of Waterloo

The Management Engineering degree is a modern take on traditional industrial engineering. At its core, management engineering is about making systems and process more effective and more efficient. It pulls from three core areas of specialization: operations research, information systems, and management of technology. The degree is extremly versatile in nature, and I ultimately choose the program because I knew I would be able to apply the principles no matter where I end up working over the course of my career.
